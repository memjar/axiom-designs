## 2. GOVERNMENT / DEFENSE DEPLOYMENTS {#government-defense}

### 2.1 U.S. Army

#### TITAN (Tactical Intelligence Targeting Access Node)
- **Platform:** Gotham + AIP
- **Contract Value:** $178.4 million (March 2024)
- **Scope:** 10 TITAN systems — Base versions (joint light tactical vehicles) + Advanced versions (tactical trucks)
- **What it does:** Replaces aging targeting systems; uses AI to analyze sensor networks, satellite imagery, drone feeds, and real-time data to provide commanders with faster targeting options
- **Status:** First TITAN Basic + Second TITAN Advanced prototypes delivered on-time (March 2025); production decision expected FY 2026
- **Significance:** First fully AI-native ground-based targeting system for the US Army

#### MAVEN Smart System (Army)
- **Platform:** Foundry + AIP + Gotham
- **Original Contract:** $480 million, 5-year IDIQ (May 2024)
- **Contract Increase:** +$795 million (May 2025, total now $1.275B+)
- **Active Users:** 20,000+ military/combatant command users (more than doubled since Jan 2025)
- **Combatant Commands Covered:** CENTCOM, EUCOM, INDOPACOM, NORTHCOM/NORAD, TRANSCOM
- **What it does:** Generative AI + ML + LLMs to analyze and visualize multi-source intelligence data; commanders receive decision support at battlefield speed

#### Army Vantage (Enterprise Data Platform)
- **Platform:** Foundry
- **Contract Value:** $400.7 million (4-year contract, up to $620M with options)
- **Active Users:** 100,000+ Army personnel
- **What it does:** Central Army data platform; open data infrastructure supporting the Army Data Platform vision; AI-enabled analytics across the enterprise

#### $10 Billion Enterprise Agreement
- **Signed:** July/August 2025
- **Value:** $10 billion ceiling over 10 years
- **Significance:** Consolidates 75 existing Palantir contracts into one enterprise deal; authorizes all DoD agencies to purchase Palantir products under this agreement
- **Context:** Largest single software contract in Army history

### 2.4 U.S. Intelligence Community (CIA, NSA, NGA, DIA)

- **Platform:** Gotham (primary)
- **CIA:** One of Palantir's first customers (2004); CIA's venture arm **In-Q-Tel** provided a $2M seed investment to Palantir; CIA relationship has been a cornerstone of the company's identity
- **NSA:** Pilot work only; relationship ultimately did not scale — Palantir's HUMINT-focus fit awkwardly with NSA's SIGINT mission; relationship reportedly ended by 2015
- **NGA (National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency):** Hosts Project Maven as a Program of Record since 2023; Maven provides cloud infrastructure and AI powering CDAO's CJADC2 initiatives
- **FBI:** Early customer, used for financial crime and terrorism investigations
- **DHS:** Uses Foundry for homeland security operations
- **Reality:** As of 2015, CEO Alex Karp acknowledged CIA was "recalcitrant"; intelligence community relationship more complex than public perception — HUMINT agencies (CIA, FBI) are deeper partners than signals agencies (NSA)
- **Known Data Sources Integrated:** HUMINT reports, signals, open source intelligence, satellite imagery, social media data

### 2.6 NHS (UK National Health Service)

#### COVID-19 Data Store (2020–2022)
- **Platform:** Foundry (on Microsoft Azure)
- **Initial Contract:** £1 (effectively free; Palantir absorbed costs to establish relationship)
- **Subsequent Contracts:** £1 million → £23 million
- **What it did:** Centralized data from NHS 111, NHS Digital, PHE test results, hospital situational reports into single integrated platform; powered the public GOV.UK Coronavirus Dashboard and Strategic Decision Makers Dashboard; supported vaccine rollout coordination
- **Partners:** Google, Amazon, Microsoft, Faculty AI, McKinsey, Deloitte all worked alongside Palantir
- **Controversy:** OpenDemocracy/Foxglove lawsuit over transparency; Palantir initially granted IP rights including ability to train models on NHS data; contracts not published until legal proceedings commenced

#### Federated Data Platform (FDP) — Current
- **Platform:** Foundry
- **Contract Value:** £330 million (7-year contract, Nov 2023); potential total value up to £500 million
- **Scale:** ~170 NHS organizations signed up; more than two-thirds already operational
- **What it does:** Central national architecture connecting datasets across the health service; enables individual trusts and integrated care boards to build their own data platforms
- **ROI Estimate (2025):** NHS estimates Palantir data platform will deliver returns of **5x its costs**
- **Why can't NHS quit Palantir?** Deep system integration, no comparable alternative, institutional knowledge baked in

### 2.7 Ukraine War Support

- **Platform:** Gotham + AIP + Meta Constellation
- **Relationship Started:** CEO Alex Karp was first Western tech CEO to meet President Zelensky after Russia's invasion; embedded Palantir in Ukrainian government within 18 months
- **Agencies Using Palantir:** Ministry of Defense, Ministry of Economy, Ministry of Education + 4+ other Ukrainian agencies
- **What it does:**
  - **Targeting:** Palantir's software is "responsible for most of the targeting in Ukraine" (per CEO Karp); targeting cycles compressed from days to minutes
  - **Intelligence Fusion:** Ingests drone video, satellite imagery, intercepted communications, civilian tips, open-source data into coherent operational picture
  - **Meta Constellation:** Orchestrates swarms of commercial satellites for ISR
  - **Natural Language Queries:** AIP allows commanders to ask questions in plain language and receive actionable targeting options
  - **Artillery Accuracy:** Palantir's AI allowed Ukraine to increase accuracy, speed, and deadliness of artillery strikes (The Times, Dec 2022)
- **War Crimes Documentation:** Ukraine's Prosecutor General partnered with Palantir (2023) to process drone footage, timestamped social media posts, intercepted communications, and satellite passes as evidence
- **Data Sovereignty Concern:** Swiss Armed Forces audit found Palantir systems likely accessible to U.S. government/intelligence agencies — Switzerland discontinued Palantir use over this concern; same risk applies to Ukraine

## 5. GO-TO-MARKET: LAND AND EXPAND STRATEGY {#go-to-market}

### Evolution of Palantir's Sales Model

**Pre-2023 (Traditional):**
- Long sales cycles (12–24 months)
- Heavy reliance on forward-deployed engineers (FDEs) embedded at customer sites
- High cost-per-customer-acquisition
- Government-first, commercial slow to scale

**Post-2023 (AIP Bootcamp Model):**
- 5-day intensive workshops where prospects build real workflows on their own data
- Customers build functional AI prototypes before signing contracts
- Sales cycle compressed from 12–24 months to weeks
- ~75% conversion rate from bootcamp to paying customer
- Led by CTO Shyam Sankar and CRO Ryan Taylor

### The "Land and Expand" Mechanics

**Step 1 — Land:** Customer engages via AIP Bootcamp; builds a proof-of-concept in 5 days on their actual data; signs initial contract (often $1–5M)

**Step 2 — Expand:** Palantir's Ontology creates deep organizational integration; teams become dependent on the semantic data layer; additional departments adopt adjacent use cases; contract value grows

**Step 3 — Enterprise:** Customer consolidates multiple contracts into enterprise agreement (e.g., US Army consolidating 75 contracts into $10B EA)

**Retention Mechanics:**
- 98% customer retention rate
- 155% dollar-based net retention (customers spend 55% more in year 2 than year 1)
- Foundry creates "sticky" integration — switching costs are extreme once the Ontology is built

### AIP Bootcamp Format
- **Duration:** 5 days
- **Setting:** Palantir hosts or travels to customer site
- **Process:** Day 1 (data integration) → Day 2-3 (use case building) → Day 4 (workflow automation) → Day 5 (demo/business case)
- **Result:** Working prototype demonstrating ROI on customer's own data
- **Cost:** Relatively low initial engagement; de-risks purchase decision dramatically
- **Competitive Advantage:** C3.ai and other competitors cannot replicate the speed-to-value; Palantir's bootcamp model has become primary growth driver

### Snowflake Partnership (2024)
- AIP now runs natively on Snowflake's Data Cloud
- Allows Palantir to "land and expand" within Snowflake's massive existing customer base
- Dramatically expands total addressable market

### Analyst Recognition
- Forrester Wave Q3 2024: Named **Leader in AI/ML Software Platforms**; ranked highest for Current Offering
- Highlights: Ontology-driven approach, AI pipelining, human-machine decision-making model

## 6. PLATFORM REFERENCE GUIDE {#platforms}

### Gotham (Government/Defense)
- Purpose: Intelligence analysis, targeting, law enforcement analytics
- Primary users: CIA, FBI, US military, international intelligence agencies
- Core capability: Data fusion from heterogeneous sources; pattern-of-life analysis; link analysis; targeting workflows
- Key feature: "Dynamic Search" across classified and unclassified data simultaneously

### Foundry (Commercial/Enterprise)
- Purpose: Enterprise data operating system for commercial organizations
- Primary users: Fortune 500 companies, commercial government agencies, industrial companies
- Core capability: Connect disparate ERP/CRM/operational systems → transform data → build applications on top
- Key feature: Ontology framework (digital twin of organization's operations)

### Apollo (Deployment Infrastructure)
- Purpose: Continuous delivery platform for deploying software across any environment
- Key capability: Deploys Gotham/Foundry across cloud, hybrid, and classified (air-gapped) environments
- Relevance: Enables Palantir to operate in classified government environments AND commercial cloud simultaneously

### AIP (Artificial Intelligence Platform)
- Launched: 2023
- Purpose: Integrate LLMs and AI agents into enterprise workflows safely
- Core capability: LLMs operate against the Palantir Ontology (semantic layer) — preventing hallucinations; AI executes real business logic
- Key feature: "Bootcamp" go-to-market model; natural language interface to enterprise data
- Runs on: Foundry (commercial), Gotham (defense), and natively on Snowflake

### Meta Constellation
- Purpose: Orchestrates networks of commercial satellites for ISR (Intelligence, Surveillance, Reconnaissance)
- Known use: Ukraine war — coordinating commercial satellite passes over conflict zones
